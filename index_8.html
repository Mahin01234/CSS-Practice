A traffic jam occurs when a large number of vehicles crowd the roads, causing slow movement or complete standstills. Common causes include rush hour congestion, road construction, accidents, and traffic signal malfunctions. Traffic jams lead to wasted time, increased fuel consumption, higher pollution levels, and driver frustration. In many cities, they also reduce productivity and emergency response efficiency. Solutions such as improved public transit, carpooling, smart traffic lights, and better urban planning can help mitigate the problem.
